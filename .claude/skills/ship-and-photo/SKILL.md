# Ship and Photo

Shipping methods, packaging guidance, and photo checklists for each collectible category. The goal: items arrive safely and photos sell the item before buyers even read the description.

---

## Shipping by Category

### Trading Cards

**Plain White Envelope (PWE) — for cards under ~$20:**
- Penny sleeve → toploader → team bag (seal the team bag)
- Place in a standard envelope between two pieces of cardboard or in a rigid mailer
- Non-machinable stamp (card makes the envelope rigid)
- Cheapest method but no tracking — some risk for both parties

**Bubble Mailer with Tracking (BMWT) — for cards $20+:**
- Penny sleeve → toploader → team bag
- Place in a padded bubble mailer
- USPS First Class Package (tracking included), typically $3.50–$5.00
- This is the standard for any card with real value

**Graded slabs:**
- Wrap slab in bubble wrap (front and back padding)
- Place in a small box (not an envelope — slabs can crack)
- USPS Priority Mail or UPS Ground for items over $100
- Add signature confirmation for items over $250

**Value thresholds for shipping method:**
| Card value | Method | Tracking | Insurance |
|-----------|--------|----------|-----------|
| Under $5 | PWE | No | No |
| $5–$20 | PWE or BMWT (buyer's preference) | Optional/Yes | No |
| $20–$100 | BMWT | Yes | Optional |
| $100–$250 | Small box, Priority Mail | Yes | Yes |
| $250+ | Small box, Priority Mail | Yes + Signature | Yes |

### Sealed Card Products

**Booster boxes:**
- Wrap box in bubble wrap on all sides
- Ship in a corrugated box with 2+ inches of padding on each side
- USPS Priority Mail (flat rate medium box works for single booster boxes) or UPS Ground
- Insure for full value — crushed boxes lose significant value

**ETBs and bundles:**
- Wrap in bubble wrap, ship in a slightly larger box
- These are more fragile than booster boxes (thinner cardboard)
- Double-box if value exceeds $100

**Blister packs / single boosters:**
- Bubble mailer for 1–3 packs
- Small box with padding for larger quantities
- USPS First Class or Priority depending on weight

### Funko Pops

**Standard Pops (under $50):**
- Pop protector (soft or semi-rigid) is expected, not optional
- Wrap protected Pop in bubble wrap
- Ship in a box sized to the Pop — minimal movement inside
- USPS Priority Mail (flat rate medium box fits most Pops with padding)

**Valuable Pops ($50–$300):**
- Hard stack protector (0.50mm thick) strongly recommended
- Bubble wrap around the protector
- Ship in a snug box with crumpled paper or packing peanuts to prevent shifting
- Add fragile stickers (they don't guarantee careful handling but help)

**High-value Pops ($300+):**
- Hard stack protector required
- Double-box method: Pop in protector in inner box → inner box padded inside outer box
- UPS Ground or USPS Priority Mail with signature confirmation
- Insure for full declared value

**Key Funko shipping rules:**
- NEVER ship a Pop in just a bubble mailer — the box will get crushed
- Box condition is 50%+ of the value — protect it accordingly
- Include Pop protector even if buyer doesn't ask — it's industry standard

### Disney Pins

**Single pins or small lots (1–5 pins):**
- Pin on a piece of cardboard (poke pin posts through cardboard, secure with clutch backs)
- Wrap cardboard in bubble wrap or place in a small bubble mailer
- USPS First Class with tracking for pins over $10

**Larger lots or valuable pins:**
- Each pin individually secured on cardboard
- Layer pins between cardboard sheets (don't let pins scratch each other)
- Ship in a small box with padding
- Priority Mail for lots over $50

**LE pins or high-value singles:**
- Individual cardboard backing with bubble wrap
- Small box with padding
- Insurance and tracking required
- Signature confirmation for pins over $250

### General Shipping Guidance

**Carrier recommendations:**
- **USPS First Class Package**: Best for items under 1 lb, $3.50–$5.00, includes tracking
- **USPS Priority Mail**: 1–3 day delivery, includes $100 insurance, flat rate options simplify pricing
- **UPS Ground**: Best for heavier items or when USPS Priority is more expensive. Reliable handling
- **USPS Media Mail**: ONLY for actual media (books, CDs). Do NOT use for collectibles — it's against USPS rules and items can be opened for inspection

**Calculated vs flat rate shipping:**
- **Calculated shipping**: eBay calculates based on buyer's location. Most accurate, no surprises. Best for heavier items
- **Flat rate shipping**: You set one price. Simpler for buyers. Use USPS flat rate boxes to keep costs predictable
- **Free shipping**: Bake shipping cost into the item price. Listings with free shipping rank higher in eBay search. Best for items over $30 where the shipping cost is a small percentage of the total

**Insurance thresholds:**
- Under $50: Optional (USPS Priority includes $100 free insurance)
- $50–$250: Recommended
- $250+: Required, plus signature confirmation
- Always get insurance for fragile items regardless of value

## Photo Checklists

### General Photo Rules (All Categories)
- **Background**: Plain white or plain black. No busy patterns, no carpet shots
- **Lighting**: Natural daylight or a ring light / desk lamp with daylight bulb. Avoid harsh shadows and flash glare
- **Resolution**: Minimum 800px on shortest side, 1600px ideal. eBay allows up to 24 free photos — use as many as needed
- **Focus**: Sharp focus on the item. Use macro mode for close-ups on phones
- **No filters**: No color correction, no brightness boosting. Buyers want to see exactly what they're getting
- **Include a reference**: Ruler, coin, or common object for scale when size matters (pins, figures)
- **Show flaws**: If there's a scratch, crease, or defect — photograph it. Not showing it leads to returns

### Trading Card Photo Checklist

1. **Front full card** — flat, well-lit, no glare. This is the hero shot
2. **Back full card** — flat, same lighting
3. **Corner close-ups** — all four corners, front side. This is what graders (and buyers) scrutinize most
4. **Edge close-up** — show any whitening or edge wear
5. **Surface detail** — angle the card to show surface scratches or print lines
6. **Holo/foil detail** — angle the card to catch the holographic pattern (this sells the card)
7. **Centering** — straight-on shot showing border widths (especially important for PSA-grade discussion)
8. **Slab photo** (if graded) — front of slab showing grade label, back of slab showing cert number, any subgrade details

**Card photo tips:**
- Use a black background for holo/foil cards — makes the pattern pop
- Photograph outside of the toploader for best clarity (then re-sleeve immediately)
- If the card has a print line or surface issue, photograph it — even if minor. Better to disclose than to get a return

### Funko Pop Photo Checklist

1. **Front of box** — full box face, window clearly visible, character visible through window
2. **Back of box** — shows the Pop lineup and item number
3. **Top of box** — often forgotten, shows brand and character name
4. **Bottom of box** — shows production info, serial number
5. **Left side** — character artwork
6. **Right side** — character artwork
7. **Window close-up** — clear shot of the figure through the window
8. **Exclusive/chase sticker** — close-up of any exclusive or chase sticker (this is a key value indicator)
9. **Any damage** — close-up of any box damage: creases, dents, corner dings, window scratches
10. **Figure detail** (if out-of-box) — paint quality, any defects

**Funko photo tips:**
- Shoot at box level, not looking down — gives the most accurate perspective
- Use a light source from the front or side to avoid window glare
- If the box has a crease, photograph it from an angle that shows the depth/severity
- Always show the sticker clearly — buyers will zoom in on this

### Disney Pin Photo Checklist

1. **Pin front** — full pin, well-lit, showing all design detail and colors
2. **Pin back** — showing back stamp, pin posts, and any edition markings
3. **Back stamp close-up** — readable "© Disney" text and any other markings
4. **Pin post detail** — show the posts and clutch backs
5. **Enamel detail** — close-up showing enamel quality, any paint bleeding or gaps
6. **Size reference** — pin next to a coin or ruler (pin sizes vary widely and photos can be deceiving)
7. **Edition marking** — if LE, show the edition number on back stamp or card
8. **Any flaws** — scratches, chips, tarnishing, missing enamel

**Pin photo tips:**
- Pins are small — macro mode is essential for readable back stamps
- Avoid direct overhead light which creates glare on enamel surfaces
- Side lighting reveals enamel texture and any surface imperfections
- For pins on original cards, photograph both the pin and the card

### Sealed Product Photo Checklist

1. **Full product front** — showing set name, branding, all labeling
2. **Full product back** — showing contents list and barcode
3. **Shrink wrap detail** — close-up showing seal integrity, manufacturer logo on wrap
4. **Seal/seam close-up** — show the factory seal is intact (or note if opened/resealed)
5. **Any damage to packaging** — dents, tears in outer wrap, crushed corners
6. **Edition/print indicators** — any stamps, stickers, or markings indicating edition or print run
7. **Multiple angles** — show overall condition from several angles

**Sealed product photo tips:**
- Shrink wrap is reflective — photograph slightly off-angle to avoid flash/glare
- Buyers want to see the seal is factory-original — get a clear shot of any manufacturer stamps on the seal
- If the product has a window showing contents (like ETBs), photograph through it
